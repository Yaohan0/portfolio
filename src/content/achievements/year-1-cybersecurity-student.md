## Context

My first year focused on establishing the fundamental knowledge required for a career in cybersecurity. Beyond learning technical concepts, I strengthened my logical thinking, programming mindset, data interpretation skills, and ability to design solutions from both a technical and user perspective. These modules provided the groundwork for tackling more specialised cybersecurity topics in subsequent years.

## Areas covered

* * * **Computational Thinking**

       – Developed structured problem-solving skills by breaking down complex problems into manageable components, designing algorithms, and applying programming logic to create efficient solutions.

    * **Data Visualisation & Analytics**

       – Learnt to collect, analyse, and present data using visualisation techniques, enabling informed decision-making through meaningful insights and effective communication of data.

    * **Cybersecurity Fundamentals**

       – Built an understanding of core cybersecurity principles, including common cyber threats, network security, cryptography, access control, risk management, and security best practices.

    * **Logic & Mathematics**

       – Strengthened mathematical reasoning and logical thinking through topics such as Boolean logic, discrete mathematics, and analytical problem-solving, forming the foundation for cybersecurity concepts and computing.

    * **User Experience & Interface Design**

       – Explored the principles of user-centred design by creating intuitive, accessible, and functional digital interfaces while considering usability, user behaviour, and design thinking.
